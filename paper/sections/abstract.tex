Large language models (LLMs) can generate fluent biomedical hypotheses but cannot trace them to specific evidence---a critical gap for scientific discovery. We introduce \textbf{KG-SCoRE}, a pipeline that uses LLM-based knowledge graph (KG) verification to clean noisy triples, scaffold hypothesis generation with verified KG paths, and verify each hypothesis's faithfulness to its path. We propose \emph{faithfulness@\textit{k}}, split into \emph{strict} (fully entailed) and \emph{broad} (entailed or partially supported), validated by an independent NLI model (98\% agreement, BroadFaith@10: 0.50 NLI vs.\ 0.49 LLM). Under fair evaluation where all methods receive post-hoc path extraction, KG-SCoRE achieves StrictFaith@10 of 0.10 (vs.\ ToG's 0.05 and RoG's 0.04) and BroadFaith@10 of 0.99, while maintaining competitive recall (Hit@10 0.25 on 20 pairs; 0.35$\pm$0.00 on 20-pair multi-seed $n{=}60$, with no significant difference from RoG's 0.38, McNemar $p{=}0.83$). KG-SCoRE significantly outperforms ToG ($p{<}0.05$), Vanilla LLM ($p{<}0.0001$), and BM25-RAG ($p{<}0.001$). KG cleaning evaluation shows perfect noise recall (1.0) but low precision (0.51), explaining why aggressive cleaning improves downstream recall. Ablations reveal that ComplEx-based learned scoring \emph{harms} recall on noisy KGs (MRR=0.013), while LLM-only verification achieves both competitive recall and near-perfect traceability.
